<p><p>Magnesium-chromite bricks are commonly used in high-temperature industrial applications such as in furnaces and kilns. They can be classified into two main market types: sintering and direct-bonded. Sintering involves the process of heating and compacting the materials to form a solid mass, while direct-bonded refers to the bricks being directly bonded to the structure without the need for additional cement or binders. Both market types offer high temperature resistance and durability, making them suitable for harsh operating conditions.</p></p>
