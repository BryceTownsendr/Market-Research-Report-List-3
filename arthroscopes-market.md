<p><p>Arthroscopy is a minimally invasive surgical procedure that enables orthopedic surgeons to view, diagnose, and treat joint problems using an arthroscope. An arthroscope is a small, flexible optic tube with a light source and a camera that is inserted into the joint through a small incision. It provides high-resolution images of the joint, allowing surgeons to make accurate diagnoses and perform precise procedures.</p><p>The global arthroscopes market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 13.4% during the forecast period. The market growth is primarily driven by factors such as increasing prevalence of joint disorders, rising geriatric population, and advancements in arthroscopic technology. Additionally, the growing demand for minimally invasive surgeries and the advantages offered by arthroscopy over traditional open surgeries are further fueling market growth.</p><p>One of the significant trends in the arthroscopes market is the introduction of disposable arthroscopes. Disposable arthroscopes eliminate the risk of cross-contamination, reduce the cost associated with reprocessing, and offer convenience to surgeons. These factors are contributing to the growing adoption of disposable arthroscopes, particularly in outpatient settings.</p><p>Another emerging trend in the market is the incorporation of artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning in arthroscopic imaging systems. AI algorithms can analyze real-time arthroscopic images to assist surgeons in making better decisions during surgeries.
