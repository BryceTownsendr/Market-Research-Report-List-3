Gastrostomy feeding involves delivering nutrition directly into the stomach through a feeding tube. Jejunostomy feeding involves delivering nutrition into the jejunum, a part of the small intestine. Nasoenteric feeding involves delivering nutrition through a tube inserted through the nose and into the stomach or small intestine. Other applications of enteral feeding administration sets include delivering medication or hydration directly into the gastrointestinal tract.</p></p>
