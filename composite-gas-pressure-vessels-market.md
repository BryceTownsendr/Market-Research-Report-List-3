<p><p>Composite gas pressure vessels are commonly categorized into Type III and Type IV based on their construction and materials used. Type III vessels are made of a metal liner wrapped with carbon fiber and offer a balance between weight and cost. Type IV vessels, on the other hand, feature a full composite construction without any metal liner, providing lighter weight but at a higher cost. Both types are used in various industries such as automotive, aerospace, and alternative fuel storage due to their high strength-to-weight ratio and safety features.</p></p>
