<p><p>Antiviral drugs refer to medications used to treat viral infections. There are different types of antiviral drugs available on the market, including reverse transcriptase inhibitors, protease inhibitors, fusion inhibitors, and immune system modulators. Reverse transcriptase inhibitors work by interfering with the reverse transcriptase enzyme, essential for the replication of certain viruses. Protease inhibitors block the activity of the protease enzyme, inhibiting viral replication. Fusion inhibitors prevent viruses from entering host cells. Immune system modulators help stimulate the body's immune response against viral infections. These drugs play a crucial role in managing and treating viral diseases.</p></p>
