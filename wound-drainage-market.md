<p><p>Wound drainage refers to the removal of excess fluid, blood, or pus from a wound. There are two types of wound drainage systems: passive drains and active drains. Passive drains rely on gravity to allow fluids to flow out of the wound, while active drains use suction to assist in fluid removal. Passive drains include simple gauze dressings and rubber tubes, while active drains involve the use of suction devices or negative pressure wound therapy systems. These drainage systems help promote healing and prevent infection in wounds.</p></p>
