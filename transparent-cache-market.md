<p><p>Transparent caching is a technique used in network architecture to reduce the pressure on network servers and improve network performance by storing frequently accessed content closer to the end-users. It involves caching content at the edge of the network, typically within Internet Service Provider (ISP) networks, to reduce latency and improve throughput. Transparent cache servers identify popular or trending content and proactively fetch and store it in their cache, ensuring quick access for subsequent requests from users.</p><p>The transparent cache market is witnessing steady growth due to the increasing demand for efficient content delivery networks (CDNs) and the proliferation of video streaming services.
