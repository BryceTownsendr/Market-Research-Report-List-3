<p><p>Compostable shrink wrap is a type of packaging material that is designed to decompose naturally in composting conditions, avoiding environmental pollution. There are various market types within this industry, including PLA (Polylactic Acid), PHA (Polyhydroxyalkanoates), Starch Blends, and Others. PLA is derived from renewable sources such as corn starch and sugarcane, while PHA is produced by bacteria. Starch Blends are a combination of biodegradable starch and other polymers. The "Others" category includes alternative materials like cellulose and chitosan that offer similar compostability characteristics. These market types provide eco-friendly alternatives to traditional shrink wrap materials.</p></p>
