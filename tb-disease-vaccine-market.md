<p><p>The TB disease vaccine market can be divided into three types: prevention vaccines, post-exposure vaccines, and others. Prevention vaccines aim to protect individuals from developing tuberculosis by providing immunity against the bacteria that cause the disease. Post-exposure vaccines are administered to individuals who have been exposed to tuberculosis to prevent the development of active disease. The "others" category refers to vaccines that may have additional purposes, such as boosting the immune response or treating drug-resistant tuberculosis. These different types of vaccines cater to various stages and risks associated with tuberculosis.</p></p>
