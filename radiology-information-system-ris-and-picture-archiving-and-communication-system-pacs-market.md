<p><p>Radiology Information System (RIS) and Picture Archiving and Communication System (PACS) are two integral components in the field of radiology that help in the management and storage of medical images and patient data. </p><p>A Radiology Information System (RIS) is a software system that captures, translates, and distributes patient radiological data, including appointment scheduling, patient tracking, reporting, and billing. It integrates seamlessly with other hospital information systems to provide accurate and timely information to radiologists and other healthcare professionals.</p><p>On the other hand, a Picture Archiving and Communication System (PACS) is a medical imaging technology that enables the acquisition, storage, retrieval, and distribution of medical images from various imaging modalities. It eliminates the need for physical film and allows for the digitization of images, making it easier to store, access, and share patient data among healthcare professionals.</p><p>The Radiology Information System (RIS) and Picture Archiving and Communication System (PACS) market have witnessed significant growth in recent years.
