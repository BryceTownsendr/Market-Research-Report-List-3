<p><p>Automotive immobilizers are security devices that prevent a vehicle's engine from starting unless the correct key or key fob is present. There are two main types of markets for automotive immobilizers: OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) and Aftermarket. OEM immobilizers are installed by the vehicle manufacturer during production, while Aftermarket immobilizers are purchased and installed by vehicle owners or third-party installers post-production. Both types serve the same purpose of enhancing vehicle security by preventing unauthorized access and theft.</p></p>
