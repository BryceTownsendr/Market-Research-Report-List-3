<p><p>The food grade prebiotics market consists of various types such as inulin, fructooligosaccharide (FOS), isomaltooligosaccharide (IMO), and others. Inulin is a natural fructose polymer found in plants, known to have beneficial effects on gut health. FOS, also derived from plants, is a shorter chain of fructose molecules with similar prebiotic properties. IMO, obtained from starch, offers a low-calorie sweetening option. Other types of prebiotics include resistant starch, xylooligosaccharides, and galactooligosaccharides. All these prebiotics promote the growth of beneficial gut bacteria, aiding digestion and overall health.</p></p>
