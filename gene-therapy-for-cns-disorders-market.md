<p><p>Gene therapy for CNS disorders involves the use of genetic material to treat or manage diseases affecting the central nervous system. Ex vivo gene therapy refers to the process of modifying a patient's cells outside the body before re-administering them. In vivo gene therapy, on the other hand, involves directly administering the genetic material into the patient's body. Both methods aim to deliver functional genes to correct genetic mutations or enhance the production of therapeutic proteins, offering potential treatments for a range of CNS disorders.</p></p>
