<p><p>Intravenous Immunoglobulins (IVIG) are classified into different types based on the specific antibodies they contain: IgG, IgA, IgM, IgE, and IgD. IgG is the most common type found in IVIG products and plays a crucial role in immune response. IgA is important for mucosal immunity, while IgM is the first line of defense against infections. IgE is involved in allergic reactions, and IgD is less understood but thought to be involved in activating B cells. Each type of IVIG serves a unique function in the immune system.</p></p>
