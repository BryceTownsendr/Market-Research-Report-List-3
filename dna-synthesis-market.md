<p><p>The DNA synthesis market encompasses two primary types: oligonucleotide synthesis and gene synthesis. Oligonucleotide synthesis involves the production of short segments of DNA called oligonucleotides, which are widely used in various applications such as genetic research, therapeutic development, and diagnostic tests. On the other hand, gene synthesis involves the assembly of longer DNA sequences, typically entire genes, using chemical or enzymatic methods. This technique is crucial in synthetic biology, genetic engineering, and gene therapy research, enabling scientists to create custom-designed genetic material for various purposes.</p></p>
