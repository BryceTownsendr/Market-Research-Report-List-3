<p><p>In-situ X-ray Absorption Spectroscopy (XAS) Cell Market includes two main types: Transmission (TM-XAS) and Fluorescent (FL-XAS). In Transmission X-ray Absorption Spectroscopy, the sample is placed between the X-ray source and the detector, allowing for the measurement of X-ray absorption by the sample. In Fluorescent X-ray Absorption Spectroscopy, the sample is excited by the X-ray beam, causing it to emit fluorescent X-rays which are then detected. These techniques are commonly used in research and analysis of a variety of materials and compounds.</p></p>
